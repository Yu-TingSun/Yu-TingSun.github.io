Core Focus: DNA-Protein Interactions Under Physiological Conditions

My research investigates how proteins recognize, bind, and manipulate DNA in environments that mimic cellular conditions‚Äîincluding molecular crowding and spatial confinement.

Primary Experimental Approaches

üî¨ Single-Molecule Techniques

TIRF Microscopy (Total Internal Reflection Fluorescence)

  • Surface-based single-molecule observation
  • Real-time monitoring of biomolecular interactions
  • Sub-second temporal resolution
  • Nanometer spatial precision

Applications in my research:

  • DNA-protein binding kinetics
  • Protein search mechanisms on DNA
  • Conformational dynamics

FRET (Fluorescence Resonance Energy Transfer)

  • Distance measurements at molecular scale
  • 2-10 nm distance range sensitivity
  • Dynamic structural changes detection

Applications in my research:

  • DNA conformational changes
  • Protein-induced DNA bending
  • Strand exchange monitoring

CoSMoS (Colocalization Single-Molecule Spectroscopy)

  • Multi-color single-molecule tracking
  • Simultaneous observation of multiple components
  • Quantitative binding kinetics

Applications in my research:

  • Protein-DNA colocalization
  • Multi-protein complex assembly
  • Substrate discrimination studies

AFM & RICM

  • AFM (Atomic Force Microscopy): Nanoscale topography
  • RICM (Reflection Interference Contrast Microscopy): Membrane-surface interactions

Applications in my research:

  • DNA carpet characterization
  • Surface-bound biomolecule imaging
  • Confinement geometry validation

Nanofluidic Channels (50-500 nm)

DNA Confinement Studies

Physics of Confinement:

  • Strong confinement: Channel size < persistence length (~50 nm)
  • Moderate confinement: Channel size ‚âà persistence length
  • Weak confinement: Channel size > persistence length

Research applications:

  • DNA extension and compaction
  • Topological constraints
  • Chromatin-mimicking environments

Microfluidic Channels (0.5-2 Œºm)

Protein-DNA Interaction Analysis

Advantages:

  • Precise control of reaction conditions
  • Rapid buffer exchange (< 1 sec)
  • Minimal sample consumption (nL volumes)
  • Elimination of surface artifacts

Research applications:

  • Competition assays (p53 vs histones)
  • Protein exchange kinetics
  • Multi-component reactions

Flow Cells

Controlled Biomolecular Reactions

Design features:

  • Multi-inlet configuration
  • Laminar flow control
  • Temperature regulation
  • Compatible with microscopy

Research applications:

  • RecA filament assembly
  • Strand exchange reactions
  • Protein titration experiments

Multi-Reservoir Systems

Molecular Crowding Simulation

Physiological relevance:

  • Cellular crowding: 300-400 mg/mL macromolecules
  • Excluded volume effects
  • Altered reaction kinetics and equilibria

Crowding agents used:

  • PEG (polyethylene glycol)
  • Ficoll
  • Dextran
  • BSA (bovine serum albumin)

Research applications:

  • Crowding effects on binding affinities
  • Protein-DNA competition under crowding
  • Chromatin dynamics in physiological environments

üß¨ DNA-Protein Systems of Interest

Recombinases

RecA, RAD51, Dmc1, and Accessory Proteins

RecA (Bacterial Recombinase)

  • Function: Homologous recombination and DNA repair
  • My focus: RNA vs DNA substrate discrimination
  • Key findings: 7-fold binding affinity difference

RAD51 (Eukaryotic Homolog)

  • Function: Meiotic and mitotic recombination
  • Research interest: Mechanistic similarities/differences with RecA
  • Potential applications: Cancer therapy targeting

Accessory Proteins

  • RecFOR: Mediator proteins
  • RecBCD: DNA processing
  • SSB: Single-strand binding protein

Research questions:

  • How do accessory proteins modulate RecA activity?
  • What determines substrate specificity?
  • How does the cellular environment affect function?

Transcription Factors

p53-Chromatin Interactions

p53 (Tumor Suppressor)

  • Function: DNA damage response and apoptosis regulation
  • Challenge: Accessing chromatin-embedded target sequences
  • My approach: Competition dynamics with histones

Why p53-Histone Competition?

  1. Clinical relevance: p53 mutations in ~50% of cancers
  2. Mechanistic gap: How does p53 compete with histones?
  3. Therapeutic potential: Enhancing p53 access for cancer treatment

Research questions:

  • How does crowding affect p53-DNA binding?
  • Can p53 displace histones from nucleosomes?
  • What physical parameters govern accessibility?

Chromatin Proteins

Histone-DNA Competition Dynamics

Core Histones (H2A, H2B, H3, H4)

  • Function: DNA packaging into nucleosomes
  • Structure: Octamer with 147 bp DNA wrapped
  • Dynamics: Breathing, sliding, eviction

Research Focus: Competition Mechanisms

Central questions:

  • How do transcription factors access nucleosomal DNA?
  • What role does molecular crowding play?
  • How does spatial confinement affect competition?

Experimental approach:

  • Single-molecule observation of competition
  • Quantitative analysis of binding kinetics
  • Physiologically relevant conditions

DNA/RNA Substrates

Strand Exchange Mechanisms

Substrate Varieties

  • ssDNA (single-stranded DNA)
  • dsDNA (double-stranded DNA)
  • ssRNA (single-stranded RNA)
  • dsRNA (double-stranded RNA)
  • Hybrid substrates (DNA-RNA hybrids)

RecA Substrate Specificity

My key findings:

  • RecA preferentially binds ssDNA over ssRNA
  • Discrimination occurs at multiple reaction stages
  • RNA-containing products have reduced efficiency

Mechanistic insights:

  • Structural recognition features
  • Binding thermodynamics
  • Kinetic barriers

Future Directions: p53 Substrate Recognition

  • How does p53 recognize specific DNA sequences?
  • Does chromatin modify p53 sequence specificity?
  • Can substrate engineering enhance p53 binding?
