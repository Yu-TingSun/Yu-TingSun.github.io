Project 1: RecA-RNA Interactions in Homologous Recombination

Status: Manuscript in Preparation (2025)

Research Question

How does RecA distinguish between DNA and RNA substrates during homologous recombination?

Objectives

  1. Quantify RecA binding affinities to different nucleic acid substrates
  2. Elucidate the molecular mechanisms of substrate discrimination
  3. Understand the functional implications for cellular recombination processes

Key Findings (To Date)

Binding Affinity Differences

  • ssDNA: Kd = 0.162 ¬± 0.03 ŒºM (strong binding)
  • ssRNA: Kd = 1.119 ¬± 0.194 ŒºM (weaker binding)
  • ~7-fold difference in binding affinity

Mechanistic Insights

  • RecA discriminates RNA at multiple stages of the reaction
  • Different mechanisms operate at different steps
  • RNA-containing products show reduced strand exchange efficiency

Kinetic Analysis

  • Identified two-step kinetic model
  • Characterized presynaptic and postsynaptic states
  • Measured distinct dwell times for each state

Techniques Employed

  • FRET (Fluorescence Resonance Energy Transfer)

    • Real-time monitoring of substrate binding
    • Distance measurements during strand exchange

  • CoSMoS (Colocalization Single-Molecule Spectroscopy)

    • Individual molecule tracking
    • Quantitative binding kinetics

  • EMSA (Electrophoretic Mobility Shift Assay)

    • Validation of binding interactions
    • Product analysis

  • Microfluidic Channels

    • Controlled reaction conditions
    • Elimination of surface effects

Significance

  • Fundamental Biology: Understanding substrate specificity in recombination
  • Evolution: Insights into RNA world to DNA world transition
  • Applications: Potential therapeutic targeting of recombination pathways

Publications

  • First co-author manuscript in preparation (2025)
  • Contributing author: Nature Communications 15(1):9266 (2024)

Project 2: p53-Histone Competition Under Physiological Conditions

Status: Proposed Research Direction

Research Question

How does p53 access chromatin-embedded DNA targets under physiological crowding and confinement?

Background & Motivation

The Challenge

  • p53 needs to bind specific DNA sequences to activate tumor suppressor genes
  • Histones package DNA into chromatin, restricting accessibility
  • Cellular environment features molecular crowding (300-400 mg/mL) and spatial constraints

Current Knowledge Gaps

  • Most studies use simplified in vitro conditions (dilute solutions, naked DNA)
  • Competition dynamics between p53 and histones poorly understood
  • Physiological factors (crowding, confinement) effects unknown

Proposed Approach

Experimental Platform

Integrated Micro/Nanofluidic System

  • Nanofluidic channels (50 nm - 2 Œºm) for spatial confinement
  • Multi-reservoir design for controlled crowding agent delivery
  • TIRF microscopy for single-molecule observation

Research Stages

Stage 1: Individual Characterization

  • p53 binding to naked DNA under crowding
  • Histone-DNA interactions in confinement
  • Establish baseline parameters

Stage 2: Competition Dynamics

  • Direct observation of p53-histone competition
  • Quantify binding affinities under physiological conditions
  • Map accessibility landscapes

Stage 3: Therapeutic Applications

  • Screen compounds that enhance p53 access
  • Develop quantitative assays for drug discovery
  • Validate findings with cell-based assays

Why This Approach is Unique

Integration of Multiple Factors

Most studies examine one variable at a time:

  • Crowding studies typically use bulk measurements
  • Confinement studies rarely include protein competition
  • Competition assays usually employ simplified conditions

This project combines:

  • ‚úÖ Single-molecule resolution
  • ‚úÖ Physiological crowding
  • ‚úÖ Spatial confinement
  • ‚úÖ Multi-protein competition

Technical Advantages

  • Established platforms from DNA confinement work (2008-2014)
  • Proven methods from RecA studies (2011-2025)
  • Immediate implementation - no new technique development required

Expected Outcomes

Fundamental Science

  • Quantitative model of competitive binding under physiological conditions
  • Understanding of how physical environment affects transcription factor access
  • Insights into chromatin regulation mechanisms

Methodological Innovation

  • New paradigm for studying multi-protein systems
  • Validated platform for complex biomolecular interactions
  • Scalable approach for different protein-DNA systems

Translational Impact

  • Drug screening platform for p53-targeted cancer therapy
  • Rational design of chromatin-modulating compounds
  • Potential for personalized medicine applications

Timeline

  • Year 1: Platform optimization and individual protein studies
  • Year 2: Competition dynamics and mechanism analysis
  • Year 3: Therapeutic compound screening and validation

Funding Opportunities

  • Cancer research foundations
  • Single-molecule biophysics programs
  • Drug discovery initiatives
  • Cross-disciplinary science grants
